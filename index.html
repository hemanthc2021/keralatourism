Theyyam
A Journey of Discovery and Enchantment

Theyyam is a traditional ritualistic performance art form of Kerala, India, known for its vibrant and visually striking costumes, energetic dances, and spiritual significance. It is a form of worship, primarily practiced in the northern regions of Kerala, and is an important cultural and religious tradition for the people of Kerala. The word "Theyyam" itself is believed to be derived from "Daivam," meaning "god" or "deity" in Malayalam, signifying that the performers embody divine beings during the ritual.

YOGA
What is yoga

Yoga is an ancient practice that originated in India thousands of years ago, combining physical postures, breath control, meditation, and ethical principles to promote holistic well-being. The word "yoga" comes from the Sanskrit root "yuj," meaning "to unite" or "to join," symbolizing the union of body, mind, and spirit. Yoga offers a wide range of benefits, from improving flexibility and strength to reducing stress and enhancing mental clarity. The practice encourages mindfulness and self-awareness, fostering a deep connection between the practitioner and the present moment. While yoga has roots in spiritual traditions, it has evolved into a widely practiced discipline across the world, accessible to people of all ages and fitness levels. Through regular practice, yoga helps to cultivate balance, inner peace, and a sense of harmony in daily life, making it a powerful tool for both physical health and emotional well-being.

ECOTOURISM

The Kerala Responsible Tourism Mission Society (KRTM Society) is the autonomous society working under Department of Tourism, Government of Kerala to spread and implement the ideologies and initiatives of Responsible Tourism all over the State. The Society has a Governing Body with the Tourism Minister as Chairman, Executive Committee, State and District level offices, and an autonomous authority.
